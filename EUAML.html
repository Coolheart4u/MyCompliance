EU's New Anti-Money Laundering Legislation

Key Regulations: AMLR (Regulation), AMLD6 (Directive), AMLAR (Authority Regulation)

Introduction to Anti-Money Laundering Legislation

The EU has introduced new anti-money laundering (AML) legislation to better prevent money laundering and terrorist financing across member states. The aim is to ensure financial security and comply with international standards.

AMLR (Anti-Money Laundering Regulation)

The AMLR is a regulation that applies directly to all EU member states without the need for transposition into national law.

  • Key Features:
  • Mandatory UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Ownership) Registers
  • Cross-Border Enforcement of Anti-Money Laundering Rules
  • Risk-Based Approach to Customer Due Diligence

AMLD6 (Anti-Money Laundering Directive 6)

The AMLD6 is a directive, which requires EU member states to transpose its provisions into national law.

  • Key Features:
  • Expansion of Predicate Offences
  • Corporate Criminal Liability
  • Stronger Penalties for Offenders
  • Enhanced Cross-Border Cooperation

AMLAR (Anti-Money Laundering Authority Regulation)

The AMLAR establishes a European supervisory authority to oversee the implementation of AML regulations across the EU.

  • Key Features:
  • Centralized Supervision of AML Compliance
  • Assistance to National Regulators
  • Guidance to Financial Institutions

Comparison of AMLR, AMLD6, and AMLAR

Aspect AMLR (Regulation) AMLD6 (Directive) AMLAR (Authority Regulation)
Type Regulation (Directly applicable) Directive (Transposition required) Regulation (Defines authority)
Scope Cross-border enforcement, UBO registers Expansion of predicate offences, corporate liability Supervision and guidance for national authorities
Main Focus Binding, uniform rules Enhanced cooperation, extended offences Creation of a central AML authority
Implementation Directly affects all EU member states National level implementation required Establishment of a European authority

Predicate Offences

The following crimes are considered predicate offences under AMLD6, and are now subject to reporting and enhanced due diligence:

  • Drug Trafficking
  • Tax Evasion
  • Corruption
  • Fraud
  • Environmental Crimes
  • Terrorism Financing
  • Human Trafficking
  • Cybercrime
  • Insider Trading

Conclusion

The new EU anti-money laundering regulations (AMLR, AMLD6, and AMLAR) are designed to enhance transparency, prevent illicit financial flows, and increase accountability for businesses and individuals involved in money laundering.

Businesses must ensure compliance with these regulations or face serious penalties. The EU aims for a unified approach across all member states to combat financial crime.
