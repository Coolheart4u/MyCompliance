AML Gap Analysis & Implementation Roadmap

Introduction

This document provides a comprehensive gap analysis and actionable roadmap for aligning current AML (Anti-Money Laundering) procedures with the updated directive issued by the Central Bank of Cyprus (CBC). The directive emphasizes a risk-based approach to client updates, simplified document requirements, and improved accessibility for all clients. The goal is to enhance compliance while streamlining operations and improving the customer experience, using Lean Six Sigma methodologies.

Implementation Roadmap (DMAIC)

1. Define

  • Goal: Align KYC/AML procedures with CBC directive.
  • Stakeholders: Compliance, Operations, IT, Customer Service

2. Measure

  • Map current KYC update processes.
  • Track cycle times, errors, complaints.
  • Risk-tier clients (high/medium/low).

3. Analyze

  • Identify process bottlenecks.
  • Use Voice of Customer (VOC) data.
  • Benchmark EU best practices.

Key Implementation Actions

  • Risk-Based Frequency: Automate checks based on client risk. Low-risk: 3 years; High-risk: annually.
  • ID Copy Acceptance: Enable secure upload of scanned/certified copies via customer portal.
  • Use Existing Data: Implement smart forms prefilled with known information.
  • Alternative Documents for Health-Impaired: Accept caregiver confirmations or medical attestations.
  • Flexible Proof of Address: Accept e-documents from CYTA, JCC, e-government platforms.
  • 3rd-Party Certification: Accept certifications from lawyers, district offices, and digital signatures.

Control & Monitoring

  • Track KPIs: update time, timely completions, client escalations.
  • Quarterly compliance audits.
  • Post-update surveys and internal feedback channels.

Lean Six Sigma Tools

  • SIPOC, Value Stream Mapping
  • FMEA for risk anticipation
  • RPA and CRM integrations for automation

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Template

SOP Title: AML Customer Update Process
Department: Compliance & Operations
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: [Insert Date]

1. PURPOSE:
   To outline a standardized, risk-based process for customer information updates in compliance with CBC AML directives.

2. SCOPE:
   Applies to all customer types: low, medium, and high-risk.

3. RESPONSIBILITIES:
   - Compliance Officer: Oversight and audit
   - Front Office: Execution of updates
   - IT: Maintain systems and automation

4. PROCEDURE:
   4.1. Client Risk Classification (initial onboarding and annual review)
   4.2. Trigger-based Updates (e.g., large transactions, address change)
   4.3. Documentation Requirements by Risk Tier
        - Low Risk: Confirm existing data, ID copy, utility or e-government proof
        - High Risk: Full re-verification annually
   4.4. Acceptable ID and Address Proof
        - Certified copy, digital uploads, 3rd-party verification
   4.5. Accessibility Support
        - Alternative options for health-affected individuals

5. DOCUMENT RETENTION:
   Store all records in secure KYC system with timestamp.

6. MONITORING & REVIEW:
   Monthly reporting; Quarterly audits

7. EXCEPTIONS:
   Must be approved by Compliance Manager
